# Receipts, Dissent, And Evidence Narrative — March 2026

This document defines how Aragora should explain its outputs to a first-time
buyer or operator without assuming deep technical context.

## The Core Story

Aragora should not ask people to trust an AI answer on faith.

It should say, in plain language:

**Aragora turns AI-assisted decisions into something you can review before you
act.**

That explanation rests on three concepts:

- **Receipt:** the record of what happened, what Aragora recommends, and what the next step is
- **Evidence:** the materials and checks Aragora relied on
- **Dissent:** the parts the system could not fully reconcile and wants a human to see

The important move is to explain these as operating concepts, not technical
features. A first-time reader does not need "provenance chain" or
"cryptographic receipt" in the opening sentence. They need a legible answer to:

- what happened
- why Aragora thinks this is the right move
- what could still be wrong
- what they should do next

## Plain-Language Framing

Recommended one-sentence explanation:

**Aragora gives every consequential AI decision a receipt: what it recommended,
what evidence it used, and where disagreement still remains.**

Recommended two-sentence explanation:

**Aragora is the review layer for AI-assisted work. Instead of asking you to
trust one model's answer, it shows the recommendation, the supporting evidence,
and the dissent before anyone acts on it.**

## Mental Model

Use a familiar business analogy:

**A receipt tells you what happened. Evidence is the supporting paperwork.
Dissent is the exception note telling you what still needs judgment.**

This works better than abstract AI language because it makes the output feel
like an operational handoff rather than a research artifact.

## Narrative For First-Time Buyers

Buyers care about trust, accountability, and adoption risk. Lead with the
business problem first:

**Today's AI tools give answers, but not enough explanation to defend a real
decision later. Aragora adds the missing layer: a receipt you can review,
share, and audit.**

What buyers should understand quickly:

- Aragora does not hide disagreement to make automation look smooth
- Aragora makes evidence visible before work is approved or published
- Aragora makes it cheaper to review consequential AI-assisted work
- Aragora gives teams a reusable record instead of an ephemeral chat transcript

Good buyer language:

- "reviewable decision package"
- "record of what happened"
- "why the system recommended this"
- "where human judgment is still needed"
- "audit-ready when you need it"

Avoid leading with:

- "cryptographic"
- "heterogeneous multi-agent debate"
- "provenance chain"
- "calibration scoring"

Those details matter later, but they should support the story, not carry it.

## Narrative For First-Time Operators

Operators care about whether they can safely move forward.

The operator narrative should be:

**You are not being asked to trust the system blindly. Aragora shows the
recommendation, the evidence behind it, and the dissent you should review
before approving, escalating, or stopping.**

A good operator-facing receipt should let someone quickly answer:

1. What is Aragora recommending?
2. What did it look at or verify?
3. Where do reviewers or models disagree?
4. Do I approve, ask for more evidence, or escalate?

Operator-friendly definitions:

- **Receipt:** the handoff summary
- **Evidence:** the basis for the recommendation
- **Dissent:** the warning label on unresolved risk

The key operator message is:

**Dissent is a safety feature, not a system failure.**

## Messaging Rules

Use these rules consistently across product, sales, and docs:

1. Start with the review problem, not the model architecture.
2. Define receipt, evidence, and dissent in plain English before using internal jargon.
3. Present dissent as useful signal, not embarrassment or noise.
4. Never imply certainty when the system is actually surfacing uncertainty.
5. Always end with the next human action: approve, escalate, request more evidence, or stop.

## Reusable Copy Blocks

Homepage / one-liner:

**Aragora gives every consequential AI decision a receipt: what happened, what
evidence supported it, and where dissent still needs human judgment.**

Sales / partner brief:

**Most AI tools produce answers you cannot defend later. Aragora produces a
reviewable receipt that shows the recommendation, the evidence behind it, and
the disagreements that still matter.**

Operator tooltip / UI helper:

- **Receipt:** what Aragora concluded and what happens next
- **Evidence:** the files, checks, and prior context used to reach that conclusion
- **Dissent:** the disagreements or risks you should review before acting

## Strategic Implication

Aragora should be presented as a system that makes AI-assisted work legible,
not merely a system that makes AI-assisted work faster.

That distinction matters because first-time buyers and operators rarely ask
"How many models are debating?" They ask:

- can I understand this decision
- can I explain it to someone else
- can I see what is still uncertain
- can I act on it safely

The narrative should answer those questions directly.
